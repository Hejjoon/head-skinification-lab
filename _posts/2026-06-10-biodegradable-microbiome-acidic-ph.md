You wash your hair with standard, high-foaming shampoos, watching the thick lather run down the drain. You assume that because the shampoo clears away grease, it must be good for your scalp. However, a few hours later, a subtle itch develops on your crown, and your scalp feels slightly irritated. You wonder why your cleansing routine causes discomfort.

To clean their hair, many people use strong, high-pH chemical cleansers daily. They believe stripping away all microbes and oils is the only way to stay clean. This is a severe mistake. The scalp is a living, biodegradable ecosystem of microbes and lipids. Using harsh chemical detergents disrupts this fragile microflora, stripping the protective acid mantle and encouraging the growth of irritating yeast populations. Furthermore, if symptoms persist or worsen, consulting a dermatologist is highly recommended.

The scalp is a breathing biological shield that requires a balanced, slightly acidic environment to support its natural defense systems.

The scalp microflora thrives in a weak-acidic environment (around pH 5.5). Using biodegradable, pH-balanced cleansing ingredients protects this acid mantle. It cleanses the scalp gently without leaving chemical residues that degrade the stratum corneum, helping to maintain a healthy microbial equilibrium.
