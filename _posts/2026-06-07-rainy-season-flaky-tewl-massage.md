During the humid rainy season, your scalp feels constantly damp and sticky. You brush your shoulders and notice white, flaky build-up on your clothes. You feel embarrassed and frustrated by this sudden onset of flakes despite the humid air.

To clear these flakes, many people use plastic scalp brushes. They scrape their skin with hard bristles during shampooing. This is a destructive habit. Scratching the humidified, softened stratum corneum tears the cellular junctions, causing rapid moisture loss.

The outermost layer of the scalp is a highly sensitive physical shield. It is not an inanimate coating. It controls the rate at which water evaporates from the body into the atmosphere.

High humidity swells the outer skin layers, making them highly vulnerable to mechanical friction. Scratching triggers spikes in transepidermal water loss (TEWL). As water escapes, the dehydrated skin cells peel off in large flakes. A gentle finger-pad massage removes loose flakes safely. By using only the soft pads of your fingertips, you cleanse the hair roots without breaking the delicate cellular junctions.
