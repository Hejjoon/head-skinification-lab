You step off a crowded subway after a long rush hour commute. The air inside was humid, sweaty, and thick with environmental dust. You run your fingers through your hair and feel a heavy layer of grease at the roots. Your immediate instinct when you get home is to squeeze a large amount of shampoo and start scrubbing vigorously.

To clear this heavy subway buildup, many people apply shampoo directly to dry or barely damp hair and scrub immediately. They believe thick lather is the only way to dissolve heavy sebum. This is a common misconception. Applying concentrated surfactants to poorly saturated hair strips away essential protective lipids and leaves surfactant residues behind, causing irritation and dryness.

The scalp is a delicate biological system that requires gentle preparation, not harsh stripping. It needs to be hydrated before cleansing agents are introduced.

A simple, 3-minute warm water pre-wash clears up to 80% of water-soluble dust and softens heavy oils. Using water heated to approximately 37°C before applying shampoo allows the heat to melt surface sebum naturally. This pre-wash saturates the hair follicles, allowing a minimal amount of weak-acidic shampoo to create a rich lather and wash away the grease without irritating the stratum corneum.
