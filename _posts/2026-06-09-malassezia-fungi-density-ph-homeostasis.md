You walk into the office on a warm, humid afternoon. The air is sticky, and your hair feels damp at the roots. Within hours, a nagging itch develops on your crown, accompanied by a greasy sensation. You feel a sudden urge to scratch, but you know that doing so will only make it worse. You wonder why your scalp feels so reactive in hot weather.

To combat this sticky itching, many people use aggressive chemical scaling products or wash their hair with harsh, highly alkaline clarifying shampoos. They believe deep chemical stripping is the only way to clean the scalp. This is a counterproductive reaction. High pH alkaline soaps disrupt the scalp's natural acid mantle, creating a breeding ground for yeast overgrowth and micro-inflammation. Furthermore, if symptoms persist or worsen, consulting a dermatologist is highly recommended.

The scalp is a biological microflora ecosystem, not a surface to be sterilized. It requires a stable, slightly acidic pH to keep its yeast populations in check.

Itchiness and grease are often linked to Malassezia yeast overgrowth. These fungi feed on scalp lipids, breaking down sebum triglycerides into irritating free fatty acids. They thrive in warm, moist, alkaline environments. Maintaining a weak-acidic environment (around pH 5.5) protects the acid mantle. Using a pH 5.5 balanced wash slows down Malassezia activity, helping to maintain a calm, healthy microbial balance on the scalp.
