It is 5 PM on your evening commute. You glance at your reflection in a shop window and notice your bangs. They are clumped, oily, and split. Just hours ago, they were fresh and dry. You feel frustrated by this daily degradation of your appearance.

To fix greasy bangs, many people apply dry shampoo repeatedly. They dust chemical powders onto the hair root. This is a temporary cosmetic fix. Dry powder absorbs oil but clogs hair follicles. More importantly, it locks in sweat and bacteria, raising the scalp's pH level.

The forehead and hairline are highly active sebaceous zones. They are not isolated surfaces. They represent a delicate boundary where skin meets hair. Maintaining a healthy acidic environment is critical for preventing excess oil production.

Healthy scalp skin maintains a pH value of 5.5. This weak acidity forms a chemical barrier against environmental stressors. If chemical residues remain, the pH climbs toward alkalinization. This shift triggers a surge in sebum output. Precision rinsing—spending three times longer rinsing than washing—clears surfactant residues. It allows the skin to maintain its natural pH 5.5 homeostasis.
